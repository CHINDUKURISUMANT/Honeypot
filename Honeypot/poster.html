Behaviour-Aware Honeypot

Adaptive Deception and Behavioural Threat Intelligence

School of Computer Science and Engineering – Winter Semester 2025 - 26

VIT UNIVERSITY

Introduction

The Behaviour-Aware Honeypot is an advanced security system designed to expose real network services (SSH and HTTP) in a safe, isolated environment.

Unlike traditional static honeypots, this system observes attacker behaviour at the session level, classifies patterns probabilistically, and dynamically adapts its deception strategies to maximize intelligence gathering.

Motivation

Traditional honeypots are often easily identifiable due to their static responses. Modern threats require systems that can:

  • Adapt in Real-Time to attacker tactics mid-session.
  • Gather Behavioural Evidence beyond simple signature-based logging.
  • Maintain Integrity of forensic data for academic and professional analysis.

Project Scope

Protocol SSH & HTTP
Isolation Docker-Based
Forensics SHA-256 Hashing

The scope encompasses the architectural design of a core orchestrator, real-time behaviour engine, and an adaptive deception layer integrated with a centralized dashboard.

Methodology

Figure 1: Adaptive Deception Architecture Concept

  • Trap Layer: Dockerized services capturing raw protocol interactions.
  • Behaviour Engine: Extracts timing, sequence, and tool-based patterns.
  • Adaptation Engine: Probabilistic selection of deception strategies (e.g., tarpitting, verbose error injection).

Key Results

Figure 2: Classification Accuracy vs. Session Duration

System validation through red-teaming scenarios demonstrates successful identification of automated scanners vs. human-driven manual exploration.

> 92% Accuracy
~500ms SSH Response
Zero Host Leakage

Conclusion

This project proves the feasibility of responsive deception. By shifting from static traps to adaptive behavioural environments, we can significantly increase the "cost of attack" for adversaries while maintaining high-fidelity threat intelligence.

Future work involves scaling the behaviour engine with deep reinforcement learning and expanding supported protocols to include RDP and SMB.
